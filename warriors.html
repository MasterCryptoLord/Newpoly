GODS

Unleash the Power of Norse Gods in Polygöd

In the mystical realm of Polygöd, a grand saga unfolds as the divine powers of the Aesir and Vanir prepare for an epic battle. As the celestial convergence draws near, ten mighty deities, renowned as the most powerful beings across the Nine Worlds, are summoned to aid mortals in their quest for glory. Each of these godly characters possesses extraordinary abilities, destined to alter the course of the great war.

The Allfather Odin himself has a limited supply of these celestial warriors to 50, ensuring that only the most devoted and worthy champions may harness their divine might. With the aid of these gods, mortals shall carve their names into the annals of history, their feats forever etched in the ancient stones of Yggdrasil.

secure the favor of these divine beings, one must make a worthy offering. The cost of their allegiance varies from 100 pieces of gleaming gold to a staggering sum of 1,600, a price that only the most affluent and determined of mortals can afford. As the final battle of Ragnarok approaches, those who possess the divine might of these ten gods shall tip the balance of power and secure their place in the halls of Valhalla.

WARRIORS

Mortal Warriors Rise in Polygöd's Final Battle

In the mystical realm of Polygöd, a new saga has emerged as warriors from across the Nine Worlds gather to prepare for an epic battle. These warriors, though less powerful than the godly Aesir and Vanir, are more accessible and affordable to the mortal realm. As the celestial convergence approaches, a vast array of warriors, each possessing unique skills and abilities, stand ready to aid mortals in their quest for glory. With a more plentiful supply than the divine gods, there is a warrior for every aspiring hero.

Unlike the limited supply of gods, these warriors are more accessible to the masses, with their allegiance obtainable for a reasonable price. For a mere 20 pieces of gold, one can secure the aid of a capable warrior in battle. Those with greater means can enlist the aid of even more skilled and powerful warriors.

As the final battle approaches, the fate of mortals rests on the shoulders of those who can secure the aid of these warriors. Though less powerful than the gods, their combined might may still tip the balance of power and secure a place in the halls of Valhalla for those who prove themselves worthy.
